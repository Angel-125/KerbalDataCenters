“You want to scan my brain,” Mortimer Kerman, CFO of the Kerbal Space Program, said.
It was not a question.

“Ja,” Wernher von Kerman replied. “Like we did with Jeb.”

“Why.”

“We want an artificial intelligence to run our data centers,” Wernher said. “In orbit.”

Mortimer frowned. “Why in orbit.”

“Sunlight for power. Vacuum for cooling. No land, no fuel, no real estate on Kerbin,” Wernher explained. “And no Kerbals required.”

“That sounds expensive.”

“It is,” Wernher admitted. “But that is where the A.I. comes in.”

Mortimer leaned back. “And what exactly do you need this… artificial me to do.”

“Run the financial software,” Wernher said. “The kind that generates revenue.”

Wernher slid a folder across the table.

Mortimer opened it. Read. Paused. Read it again.

The room was very quiet.

Mortimer closed the folder.

“You may scan my brain.”

Thus was born MechMort, and with it the first Kerbal Data Center — orbital compute infrastructure dedicated to efficiency, automation... and profit.
